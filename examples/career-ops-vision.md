# CareerOps -- What's Possible with the Skill Ladder

**Repo:** https://github.com/santifer/career-ops

## What It Is

CareerOps is an AI-powered job search system that someone built entirely with Claude Code. It replaces the manual grind of job hunting with an intelligent pipeline.

## What It Does

- **Reads job descriptions** and scores them against your profile across 10 dimensions (A-F grading)
- **Generates customized resumes** that are ATS-optimized for each specific role
- **Scans 45+ company portals** (Anthropic, OpenAI, ElevenLabs, etc.) across Ashby, Greenhouse, Lever
- **Batch evaluates** multiple opportunities in parallel
- **Discourages bad-fit applications** -- if the score is low, it tells you not to bother

## Why It Matters

This is what the Skill Ladder looks like taken to its logical conclusion:

1. **Started as manual work**: Reading JDs, customizing CVs, tracking applications in a spreadsheet
2. **Became skills**: "Evaluate this JD against my profile" -- a single repeatable process
3. **Became agents**: Batch scanning portals, parallel evaluation
4. **Became a system**: A full pipeline that runs end-to-end

## The Leverage

Imagine you're in recruitment. Right now, you do this work for each candidate, one at a time. With a system like this:

- Each candidate gets a **personalized AI job search engine**
- What used to take hours per person becomes **minutes**
- You stop selling a service and start offering a **product**

That's the shift: from doing the work to building the system that does the work.

## You Don't Need to Build This Today

CareerOps is complex. It's the destination, not the starting point. Your starting point is one skill -- "evaluate a job description against a candidate profile." Everything else grows from there.
