# Remote Onboarding Bottleneck

## Core Insight

New hires in remote-first teams take 2.3x longer to reach full productivity compared to in-office cohorts. The bottleneck is not training content -- it's the absence of informal knowledge transfer (hallway conversations, overhearing colleagues, watching how things are done).

## Evidence

- [VERIFIED: Q1 retro data] Average time-to-productivity: 47 days remote vs 21 days in-office
- [VERIFIED: exit survey] 68% of early leavers (< 6 months) cited "feeling disconnected" as primary reason
- [INFERRED: correlation] Teams with structured buddy systems showed 34% faster ramp-up

## Implications

1. **Structured knowledge capture matters**: If informal knowledge isn't written down, remote hires can't access it
2. **Buddy systems are high-leverage**: Low cost, significant impact on ramp time
3. **Documentation is not enough**: People need context, not just information -- why decisions were made, not just what was decided
